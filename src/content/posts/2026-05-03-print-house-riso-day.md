The risograph is a strange machine. It's somewhere between a photocopier and a screen printing press, and it produces flat, slightly-misregistered, deeply colorful prints that no other process really replicates.

Yesterday we ran a 60-print edition for a local poet. Two passes — fluorescent pink first, then federal blue on top. The whole job took about ninety minutes once we'd burned the masters, and the misregistration on the bottom corner of every print is a feature, not a bug.

If you're thinking about a poster, zine, or art print and don't know where to start, send us a sketch. We'll talk you through what works on the riso and what doesn't.

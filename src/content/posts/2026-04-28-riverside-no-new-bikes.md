Every couple of weeks, somebody asks if we'd consider stocking new bikes. The answer is no, and the reason is climate, not nostalgia.

Manufacturing a new aluminum frame burns somewhere on the order of 120 kg of CO₂ before it ever touches a road. Steel is even worse. Meanwhile, there are an estimated 3 million unused bicycles sitting in American garages and storage units right now. The math doesn't favor more manufacturing.

So we focus on what's already here. We pull frames out of basements, fix what's broken, replace what's worn, and put bikes back on the road for a fraction of the cost — financial and environmental — of new.
